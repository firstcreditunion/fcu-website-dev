Common questions
Do I need a 20% deposit?
Not necessarily. While many lenders require 20%, we may be able to assess home loan applications with a smaller deposit depending on your individual circumstances. Contact us to discuss.
What is the difference between fixed and floating rates?
A floating rate can change over time but gives you flexibility to make extra repayments without penalty. A fixed rate locks in your rate for a set period (1 or 2 years), giving you repayment certainty. Early repayment fees may apply on fixed loans.
Are there any application fees?
No. First Credit Union does not charge application fees on home loans.
Can I get pre-approved?
Yes — we can pre-approve your home loan before you start looking so you know exactly how much you can spend. This is particularly useful in a competitive market.
Can I make extra repayments on a floating rate loan?
Yes. On a floating rate home loan you can make additional repayments at any time. For fixed rate loans, early repayment fees may apply if you repay before the fixed term ends.
Are these rates for investment properties too?
The rates shown apply to owner-occupied home loans only. Contact us if you are looking for investment property lending.
